  • About task panes

    Many features in Microsoft Office are hosted in task panes stationed on the side, and it is one of the most commonly used UI patterns across all Office apps.

    Task panes are not responsive and do not scale. They typically take about 20% of users' screen real estate (depending on the devices they use).

  • Role

    Rom Impas and I drove the entire design, including research, UI design, and prototyping

  • Timeline

    Design: July 2018 to Oct 2018
    Development: Ongoing

Context and challenge

Whenever users open multiple task panes at once, the panes stack right next to each other. Since our task panes are static (around 320px width), having more than two task panes opened at once pretty much takeover the entire screen, leaving little to no room to the canvas and therefore occluding a significant amount of users content.

Having 3 panes opened at the same time on a standard 15 inches laptop

A management system for task panes

Telemetry shows that only about 5% of pane sessions had 2 or more on screen simultaneously at some point, but a lot of Office's upcoming features are pane-based. We believe a pane management system can help users to preserve more screen real estate,  therefore having more productivity and fewer pane opening > closing required.

Proposing a new model

Initially another team planned to use the horizontal model for this effort. While it could be a good solution for two panes, it doesn't take many problems we have into account. That said, we explored and designed a new vertical model and attempted to get stakeholders buy-in.

Process

Learn from existing data

We utilized qualitative data we got from previous user studies (flash feedback and usertesting.com tests) and quantitative data from telemetries. We made design principles based off of what we learned there.

Design brainstorms

We had multiple design sessions to talk about design goals, pain points, challenges, timeline, and so on. We came up with mid fidelity mockups quickly.

Interaction Design & Rapid prototyping

In order to quickly visualize the model, I prototyped all the micro-interactions associated with the design. We iterated with multiple rounds of feedback and showcased our designs to team members, stakeholders, as well as managements.

Outcome
