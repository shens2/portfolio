Context and challenge

Many user workflows start with an audio recording of a conversation, interview, or meeting. ​​These recordings then get transcribed, either manually in a painstaking process that takes at minimum twice as long as the recording, or get sent off to third-party services.

They then get analyzed in places like Word and OneNote. There is opportunity to bring the user into Office sooner and solve a painful customer problem by providing our own transcription service.​

First Draft UX Walkthrough

Process

Understanding the domain

That means understanding transcription in general. How and why do people transcribe? What kind of software/services are available in the market right now?

User research with domain experts

Luckily Microsoft has a lot of professionals that regularly use transcription on a daily basis and they are willing to talk to us. We drafted up a list of questions and talked to several user researchers as well as a communication director to understand their goals and pain points.

Design explorations

Quick mockups to visualize an end-to-end user flow

User test and experiment

We run extensive user studies to validate our designs. We use usertesting.com and in person interviews interchangeably.

Outcome

This project is ongoing right now. We expect to see this feature live on Word Online the early 2020.
