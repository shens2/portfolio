The highlighted portion of the app is the "ribbon"

  • About the "Ribbon"

    The ribbon is where most of the commands are located in Office Apps. It is however, criticized heavily in the history of Office as it has way too many commands and most users don't even know certain features exist.

  • Role

    Lead Designer, working with CXE, Excel and PowerPoint

  • Timeline

    Design: April 2019 - Ongoing
    Development: Ongoing

Context and challenge

In Win32 Word, there are currently more than 100+ commands located within the Ribbon. A huge portion of the commands are rarely used and there's a clear opportunity to simplify the ribbon for the generic users. I was tasked to design the SLR (single-line ribbon) for Word Win32, while collaborating with design representatives from Excel and PowerPoint.

Design approach

Telemetry driven

Telemetry data was pulled, organized, and analyzed in order to measure what commands to prioritize or de-prioritize.

Simple and powerful

Word is the most powerful authoring tool and while we try to simplify it, we are not hindering its powerful features. In other words, we aren't just hiding commands into the overflow menu.

Cross platform / app continuity

The design should scale and work for other office apps for consistency reasons.
